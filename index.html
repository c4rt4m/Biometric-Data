Biometric Data

Defining the Topic

In today’s world, digital security plays an important role in protecting individuals from online threats. One of most serious threats within the area of digital security identity theft, which involves illegally obtaining an individual’s personal details, such as credit card numbers or social security details with the intention of impersonating them for financial or personal gain. (Baecker, 2019). Due to the evolution of technology, biometric data has become more widespread and offers features that increases protection and security.

Dantcheva (2015) defined biometrics as “the science of recognizing individuals based on their physical, behavioral, and physiological attributes such as fingers, face, iris, gait and voice” (p.1). Unlike passwords and patterns biometric traits cannot be changed, therefore, securing this data is essential in reducing the risk of identity theft.

Background

Today, biometric data is rapidly emerging as one of the most effective tools in fighting against identity theft. Unlike traditional passwords or PINs, which can be forgotten, stolen, or hacked, biometric data such as fingerprints, facial recognition, and iris scans are unique to each individual, making them harder to replicate or misuse.

This website, created by Carleen, James & Natu, explores how biometric technologies are reshaping digital security. As we move further into a data-driven world, understanding biometrics is essential to building secure, accessible systems that protect our personal identity and help mitigate identity theft.
